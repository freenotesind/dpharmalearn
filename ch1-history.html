Chapter 1 — Pharmaceutics I

History & Development of Pharmacy

Pharmacy profession in India · Education · Industry · Practice · Professional associations · Pharmacy as a career · Pharmacopoeia — IP, BP, USP, NF & Extra Pharmacopoeia

1. Definition of Pharmacy

Pharmacy is the science and art of preparing, compounding, dispensing and proper utilisation of drugs for prevention, diagnosis and treatment of disease.

The word pharmacy comes from the Greek word pharmakon meaning drug or medicine.

2. History of Pharmacy Profession in India

2.1 Ancient Period — Ayurveda

Pharmacy in India has its roots in Ayurveda (ayur = life + veda = knowledge). The ancient texts Charaka Samhita and Sushruta Samhita are the earliest records of pharmacy practice in India.

TextAuthorContribution
Charaka SamhitaCharakaInternal medicine; 341 plant, 177 animal, 64 mineral drugs
Sushruta SamhitaSushruta (Father of Indian Surgery)Surgery; 395 medicinal plants described

2.2 British Period (1757–1947)

  • Western medicine and pharmacy introduced by East India Company
  • First medical college established in Calcutta in 1835
  • Drugs and Cosmetics Act 1940 enacted to regulate manufacture and sale of drugs

2.3 Post-Independence (1947 onwards)

  • Pharmacy Act 1940 came into force in 1948
  • Pharmacy Council of India (PCI) established in 1949
  • First edition of Indian Pharmacopoeia (IP) published in 1955
  • India became the world's largest supplier of generic medicines

3. Pharmacy Education in India

Pharmacy education is regulated by PCI under the Pharmacy Act 1940.

D.Pharm — Key Facts

  • Duration: 2 years + 500 hours practical training
  • Regulated by PCI under Pharmacy Act 1940
  • Minimum qualification to register as a pharmacist in India

4. Pharmacy Industry in India

  • India is the world's largest supplier of generic medicines (20% of global generic drug volume)
  • India supplies 60% of global vaccine demand
  • India is the 3rd largest producer of drugs by volume in the world
  • Indian pharma market worth approximately USD 50 billion
  • Major sectors: Bulk drug (API) manufacturing, Formulation manufacturing, Retail pharmacy, Hospital pharmacy, R&D

8. Pharmacy Act 1940

  • Enacted: 1940 | Came into force: 1948
  • Purpose: To regulate the profession and practice of pharmacy throughout India
  • Section 3: Establishes Pharmacy Council of India (PCI)
  • Section 12: Establishes State Pharmacy Councils
  • Section 31: D.Pharm from PCI-approved institution required to register as pharmacist
  • Section 32: Penalty for practising without registration

9. Pharmacopoeia

Definition

A Pharmacopoeia is an official book published by a government or authorised body. It contains a list of drugs with their standards for identity, purity, strength and quality, along with methods of preparation, tests and storage conditions.

9.1 Indian Pharmacopoeia (IP)

  • Published by: Indian Pharmacopoeia Commission (IPC), Ghaziabad
  • Under: Ministry of Health & Family Welfare, Govt. of India
  • IPC established: 1999
  • Latest edition: 8th edition (2022)

9.2 Salient Features of IP

  • Contains standards for identity, purity, strength and quality of drugs
  • Includes monographs for drugs, dosage forms, excipients and biological products
  • Has legal force — all drugs sold in India must comply with IP standards
  • Contains general notices, tests, reagents, standard solutions and reference standards
  • Also includes Ayurvedic, Unani and Siddha (ASU) drug monographs
  • Available in print and digital formats
  • Published periodically with supplements for new drugs

9.3 British Pharmacopoeia (BP)

  • Country: United Kingdom
  • Published by: British Pharmacopoeia Commission, UK
  • First published: 1864
  • Contains standards for medicines, pharmaceutical substances and formulated preparations used in the UK
  • Widely accepted and referenced internationally

9.4 United States Pharmacopoeia (USP)

  • Country: USA
  • Published by: United States Pharmacopoeial Convention (non-profit organisation)
  • First published: 1820 — oldest pharmacopoeia in the world
  • Contains standards for drugs, dosage forms, excipients and dietary supplements
  • Combined and published together with NF as USP-NF

9.5 National Formulary (NF)

  • Country: USA
  • Published by: Published along with USP as USP-NF
  • Contains standards primarily for pharmaceutical excipients (inactive ingredients used in formulation)
  • In India, National Formulary of India (NFI) is published by IPC — contains therapeutic guidelines and drug information

9.6 European Pharmacopoeia (EP)

  • Region: Council of Europe (European countries)
  • Published by: European Directorate for Quality of Medicines (EDQM)
  • First published: 1969
  • Contains common standards for medicines used across all European member states

9.7 International Pharmacopoeia (Ph.Int.)

  • Published by: World Health Organization (WHO)
  • First published: 1951
  • Contains recommended (not legally binding) standards for drugs used globally
  • Primarily used by developing countries that do not have their own pharmacopoeia

9.8 Extra Pharmacopoeia (Martindale)

  • Full name: Martindale: The Complete Drug Reference
  • Published by: Royal Pharmaceutical Society, UK (since 1883)
  • NOT an official pharmacopoeia — it is a comprehensive drug reference book only
  • Contains drugs NOT found in official pharmacopoeias — new drugs, investigational drugs, herbal medicines
  • Has no legal force — used only as a reference guide

Key Difference — Official vs Extra Pharmacopoeia

  • Official Pharmacopoeia (IP, BP, USP): Published by government/authorised body · Has legal force · Contains official drug standards
  • Extra Pharmacopoeia (Martindale): NOT official · No legal force · Only a reference book · Contains drugs not in official pharmacopoeias

Quick Revision

  • Pharmacy = science of preparing, compounding and dispensing drugs
  • Charaka Samhita + Sushruta Samhita = earliest Indian pharmacy records
  • Pharmacy Act 1940 → force 1948 → Section 3 = PCI established
  • PCI = 1949, New Delhi = regulates pharmacy education in India
  • IPA (1939) = professional body for pharmacists in India
  • IP = Indian Pharmacopoeia | published by IPC | 8th edition (2022) latest
  • USP (1820) = oldest | BP (1864) = UK | NF = excipient standards with USP
  • Martindale = NOT official = only reference book = no legal force
